Chicken Marsala

A classic Italian dish that is a staple in restaurants the world over. While the common preparation is usually a cream-based sauce, I am opting for a more traditional approach. Laid over a bed of buccatini and paired with a crusty slice of bread, this rustic dish will be sure to please.

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken breasts
  • salt and pepper
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 small shallots, chopped
  • 1 cup chestnut mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 tbsp flour
  • 1/2 cup marsala wine
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  • flat leaf parsley

Steps

  1. Cover the chicken breasts with a cling wrap and pound them with a meat mallet until they are no thicker than Â½ inch. Sprinkle with salt and pepper on both sides and dredge in flour.
  2. Fry in olive oil until golden over medium-high heat for approximately 5 minutes. Do not over cook as the chicken breasts will be quite thin and will cook fast.
  3. Remove to a separate plate and set aside.
  4. To the same pan add chopped shallots and cook over low heat for 2-3 minutes, then add the sliced mushrooms, salt and pepper and saute for 5 minutes.
  5. Add 1 tbsp of flour, stir and cook for a minute longer, then add marsala wine and turn the heat up to medium.
  6. Add chicken stock and bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to a gentle simmer and return the chicken to the pan.
  7. Let it heat through in the sauce, simmer for a few minutes to allow the sauce to thicken to a desired consistency.
  8. Serve sprinkled with chopped parsley and a side dish of your choice.
